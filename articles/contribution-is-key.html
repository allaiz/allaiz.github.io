Contribution is Key

A Joint Venture needs a way of determining what share of profit, risk, and control each member has. In a Self Employment Joint Venture, you would ideally have a share of control that is equal to your relative contribution. The most honest measure of your contribution is the share of profit that other JV members are willing to give you. And the proportion of risk that you bear should be equal to the amount of control you had in deciding what risks were taken. Thus your profit share, risk, and control should all be proportional.


No room for salaries or wages, only profits and losses

Being self employed means having control over your business, and bearing all the reward and risks that come from it.

In the same way then, contributing to a Self Employment Joint Venture should mean that you have control over the JV in proportion to your contribution, and that you should also bear an equivalent proportion of it’s reward and risk. And what’s not included in that is any sort of fixed-amount claim on the profits of the venture, or any sort of salary or wage.

Fixed prices should be for your customers… not your colleagues. You should share the fortunes of the business together.

Goal congruence

Another big advantage of keeping things proportional is Goal Congruence. Goal congruence means everyone on the team having the same objective - they want the same thing and are motivated to work for the same goal. This naturally keeps your team coordinated and effective, and is most likely to occur when the incentives for each team member are the same - which happens when risk, reward, and control are proportional. If you start handing out fixed salaries or other risk-free income to some JV members, then this proportionality becomes distorted, and goal congruence is lost.

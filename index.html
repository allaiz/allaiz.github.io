Self Employers Working Together
to Achieve More

Many people would rather work for themselves than have a job. And many people prefer to be self employed and focus on work they love doing, rather than hiring employees and spending most of their time managing people.

Self employment has some drawbacks though. You have to do everything yourself. You have so many sales and accounting and fulfillment and support responsibilities that it can be hard to concentrate on the work that you love doing. And you can only work on small projects that don’t need a large team.

When these drawbacks start to outweigh the benefits of self employment, many self employers are reluctantly forced to either hire people or find a job.

Allaiz.com seeks to create a third option.

If self employers get together and create an incorporated joint venture that acts as a proxy for their individual businesses, they can enjoy the benefits of self employment AND of working in a team.


Join an Alliance. Make Money. Enjoy Freedom.

My name is John. I am working to make Allaiz.com into a community for people (like us) who want to create or join self employer joint ventures. Allaiz.com will also have an app that helps us coordinate joint ventures, and that automates away business administration tasks so we can focus on work we love to do.

While I’m doing this, I am writing about the process in a number of articles about self employer joint venture alliances, as well as business administration (with a focus on Australian business law and taxation), and the technology that runs Allaiz.com.

I have also set up a subreddit at r/allaiz where we can meet and organize self employment alliances in the mean time, so if that is something that interests you, come on over and say hi here! :-)

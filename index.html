This means being Self Employed, and having a Joint Venture with other people who are Self Employed.

Being “Self Employed” means having your own business but not having any employees. You are the business - you’re a one person show. Self Employed people often have their own company or other corporate structure that they conduct business through (sometimes referred to as being Self Incorporated), but they are usually the only shareholder and director of the company too.

A “Joint Venture”, or “JV” is formed when a number of different businesses (usually companies) get together to work on a particular project that they can more easily do together than separately. Usually this is because the different JV members have different skills or resources that are complimentary, and by combining them they can take advantage of business opportunities they would not have on their own.

Joint Ventures are created by making a contract between the members that sets out what each member agrees to do, how the JV business will be run, and how profits, expenses, and risks will be distributed between the different members. This contract is called a Joint Venture Agreement, or JVA for short.

A Self Employment Joint Venture (or SEJV) is a special type of JV that is for Self Employers only. You can create one by using a Joint Venture Agreement that requires all the members of the JV to be Self Employed, and that excludes any businesses that have employees. This allows the JV to conduct business without requiring anyone to be a Wage Slave. For this to work properly, the JVA should also require that any Subsidiaries of the JV and any other businesses that the JV controls or invests in should not have employees either.
